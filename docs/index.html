Father Absence & Adult Economic Outcomes

Multi-cohort analysis of associations between childhood resident biological father absence and adult earnings, employment, and household income. Primary analysis uses the NLSY97 cohort (N = 8,982), with ACS and CPS public benchmarks.

Key Findings

Large earnings gap

Father-present adults earn $70,784 on average versus $51,731 for father-absent adults—a $19,053 difference. The gap varies by race and sex: $19,853 for non-Black non-Hispanic males, $9,604 for Black females.

Explore outcomes

Prevalence varies sharply by race

65% of Black respondents, 40% of Hispanic respondents, and 33% of non-Black non-Hispanic respondents grew up without a continuous resident biological father. ACS 2024 child data shows similar gradients.

View prevalence data

Poverty gradient

ACS 2024: 59% of children below poverty lack a resident father versus 7.6% in the top income quartile. Father absence concentrates at the bottom of the income distribution.
