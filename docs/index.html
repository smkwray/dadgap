Father Absence & Adult Outcomes

Multi-cohort analysis of associations between childhood resident biological father absence and adult outcomes across cognition, education, earnings, employment, health, mental health, and family formation. Primary analysis uses the NLSY97 cohort (N = 8,982).

Key Findings

Cognitive & education gaps persist after adjustment

Father-absent respondents score 0.37 SD lower on g_proxy; 0.19 SD persists after covariate adjustment. Education gap: 1.3 years raw, 1.2 years adjusted.

Explore outcomes

Earnings gap varies by race

Overall $19,053 gap. Largest for non-Black non-Hispanic males ($19,853), smallest for Hispanic females ($6,483). Employment gap: 7.7 pp.

Explore outcomes

Steep poverty gradient

ACS 2024: 59% of children below poverty lack a resident father vs 7.6% in the top income quartile. Father absence concentrates at the bottom of the income distribution.
